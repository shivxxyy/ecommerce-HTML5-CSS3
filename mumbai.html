MARKETS IN MUMBAI

at barraging.
Location:
South Bombay

Nearest Station:
Churchgate Station

What to Buy:
Bags, Accessories, Clothes etc.

Closed On:
Mondays
Linking Road Market
Situated in the Bandra part of the Mumbai city, Linking Road Market is one of the
most loved and most popular markets in Mumbai. Best known for souvenir shopping
and also as a tourist spot, Linking Road Market is also famous as a wedding shopping
destination offering all kinds of lehenga cholis and other trinkets and accessories.
Other than regular shopping, the market is also a wholesale bazaar and extends in
the neighbourhood to Bandra Market and Book Market. The central road in Linking
Road is flanked by local stalls and shops selling all kinds of stuff from apparel to
footwear, accessories, home decor and appliances etc. You need to practising your
bargaining skills here to get a good deal and quality product.
Location:
Bandra

Nearest Metro Station:
Bandra Station

What to Buy:
Electronic Appliances, Home Accessories, Jewellery etc.

Closed On:
Monday
Crawford Market
From traditional Gujarati apparels to Tibetan goods, the Crawford Market has something
for every shopper. It opens at 10:30 AM and closes at 9 PM daily, although the Tibetan
markets are often closed on Sundays and Mondays. You will get ethnic apparel, paintings
and artworks, leather goods, embroidered handbags and jholas, Pashmina shawls, carpets,
junk jewelry, Tibetan masks, Kolhapuri chappals, brass trinkets and winter clothing at
affordable cost.
Location:
South Bombay

Nearest Metro Station:
Churchgate Station

What to Buy:
Clothes, Silver Jewellery, Paintings, Wooden Handicrafts etc.

Closed On:
Sunday
